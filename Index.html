Climate Change

About Climate Change

  • Climate change refers to the long-term alterations in Earth's climate patterns, including temperature, precipitation, and wind.
  • It is caused by human activities that release large amounts of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, primarily from the burning of fossil fuels and deforestation.
  • It is also leading to the loss of biodiversity, as ecosystems struggle to adapt to changing conditions and species are forced to migrate or go extinct.

Why should we prevent Climate Change?

  • Climate change is causing irreversible damage to our planet,including rising sea levels, increased frequency and intensity of natural disasters.
  • It is also contributing to the spread of diseases, poor air quality, and extreme weather events that threaten human health and wellbeing.
  • Preventing it can lead to increased energy efficiency and decreased energy consumption, saving money and reducing emissions.
  • By preventing climate change, we can ensure that future generations inherit a sustainable and livable planet.

Effects on plants and animals due to Natural disasters

  • Habitat destruction: Natural disasters such as wildfires,hurricanes can destroy or damage plant and animal habitats
  • Displacement and migration: When natural disasters occur, animals may be forced to flee their habitats and seek new areas for food, and shelter.
  • Species loss: Natural disasters can also lead to the loss of plant and animal species. For example, wildfires can destroy entire ecosystems and cause the loss of endangered species.
  • Soil erosion: Floods and other natural disasters can cause soil erosion, which can lead to loss of nutrients in the soil and decreased plant growth.
